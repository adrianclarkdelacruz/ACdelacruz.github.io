Data Analysis and Forecast of Covid-19 Cases in the Philippines

Background

Covid-19 is a global epidemic that greatly threatened the whole world, as it claimed many lives, but is now slowly being ignored. This observation is further supported by Google Trends, which shows that from January 2022 to October 2022 google searches about Covid-19 in the Philippines had been reduced by 91 percent.

The objective of this project is to analyze the current Covid-19 situation in the Philippines using Python. Additionally, we will also use Machine Learning algorithm to forecast the number of confirmed Covid-19 cases and offer recommendations to the public about the ongoing Covid-19 situation.


Dataset

To build our model, we will use the Covid-19 dataset from the Department of Health (DOH) between January and October 2022. The dataset consists of 993,147 rows and 22 columns, with a total of 18,200,926 data points. View Dataset


Model Diagram

We will employ this model to analyze and predict the number of daily confirmed cases of Covid-19 in the Philippines.



Conclusion and Recommendations

Using Machine Learning Algorithm, we are able to forecast that the daily confirmed cases of Covid-19 in the Philippines are likely to increase, peaking around December 1st before gradually declining. It's expected that the consolidation of cases will be higher than that of October and November 2022.

This project forewarns the policy-makers and the Filipino people, that the threat of Covid-19 in the Philippines is still present. Therefore, it is highly recommended that our policymakers to review our policy of allowing the voluntary use of facemasks in indoor and outdoor spaces. Likewise, we should remind the public to still stay vigilant and follow the safety protocols in order to prevent the recurrence of major spikes in the number of confirmed Covid-19 cases in our country.
